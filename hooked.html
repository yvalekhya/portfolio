Hooked App Redesign





Overview

The hooked app is a rewards and deals app located around college towns with the goals of helping students to find cheaper meals for select stores.




Problem Statement

Located in Davis, I have been using this app for a couple years now, with not many updates made to the app at all. The User Interface (UI) of this app is very minimalist and easy to read, however, some interfaces, such as the map view, are hard to distinguish. For example, when I want to view "hooked deals" near me, it displays the icons but it does not allow you to click on the icons to view which companies are located there. Instead, they have a left-to-right swipe image bar on the bottom of the screen in hierarchy of distance from the user. Although this interface is organized efficiently, I found it inconvenient for users to be able to pick which companies they specifically would like to choose to go to. Apart from this, I have also noticed that the app does not allow for filters. Which is a bummer, since every great product design incorporates user choices. The filters are very limited to the product, only offering location and time as choices to find "hooked deals." Filters that I find important to incorporate include: choices of cuisine, specific company searches, and distance. Although the concept behind this app is great, many people that I have interviewed agreed that this app is very limited and for picky eaters especially, this app is not efficiently usable. In my redesign, I chose to create a hamburger menu on the top right, to be able to create a new page of filters, and be able to customize what filters are most wanted, and having the ability to change it.
